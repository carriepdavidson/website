The gentle pause

In for four, out for six, five rounds. Follow the circle with your belly, not your shoulders. This is the smallest version of what we practice together.

Press begin

If breathing is out of reach

A flooded body cannot count breaths. Go outside-in instead:

  • Cold water on your face, or an ice cube in your fist.
  • Look: name five things you can see, out loud.
  • Move: push against a wall, shake, walk fast.
  • Hum: one long, low note.
  • Borrow calm: a voice you trust, a pet under your hand.

Build your kit while you’re settled; nobody chooses tools mid-wave. This is education, not medical care. If you are in crisis, the resources in the footer are there for you.

The honest fit

Is this the right fit?

Choose this work when

You are stable, self-aware, and still stuck in a loop you understand. Willingness to look at every version of yourself, with curiosity rather than judgment, is the only entry requirement.

Not the right season when

You are in acute crisis, or you need diagnosis or treatment first; licensed care comes before coaching, and I will say so plainly. And it is not a fit if you want the pattern named but not changed.

Is this therapy?

No. Therapy looks back to diagnose and treat. I work present-and-forward: patterns, regulation, creation. My coaching can walk beside therapy without replacing it.

My work is LGBTQ+ affirming and inclusive of all identities and relationship structures.

Questions, answered plainly

The honest fine print

How much does trauma recovery coaching cost?

The ARC Program, 12 weeks of private coaching, is $3,000, paid in full or as three monthly payments of $1,000. The total is the same either way. Rooted & Rising, the self-paced course, is $222. The Monthly Gathering is free.

Who is this work not for?

This work asks for honesty and willingness. It is not the right season if you are in acute crisis, or if you need diagnosis or treatment first; licensed care comes before coaching, and I will say so plainly. And it is not a fit if you want the pattern named but not changed. Willingness to look at every version of yourself, with curiosity rather than judgment, is the only entry requirement.

What is the difference between a trauma coach and a therapist?

A therapist diagnoses and treats mental health conditions, often working through the past. As a coach I work present-and-forward on patterns, regulation, and creation. I am a registered nurse, not a therapist, and I refer out when someone needs clinical care.

What if I am not ready for coaching yet?

Start free. Take the three-minute pattern quiz, download the Trauma Tree Workbook, or join the Sunday Letter. The door to the deeper work stays open, and there is no clock on it.

If you are in crisis, reach out now

You deserve immediate support. In the U.S.: call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) · text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line) · SAMHSA National Helpline 1-800-662-4357. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

Coaching is companionship alongside care, not a replacement for it. This work is trauma recovery coaching and education. It is not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment, and it is not a substitute for care from a licensed mental-health or medical professional. If you are working with a therapist or physician, coaching can walk beside that care. It does not replace it.
