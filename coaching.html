The short version

What is a trauma recovery coach?

A trauma recovery coach is a trained, non-clinical guide who helps you move present-and-forward: interrupting the patterns trauma left behind and building new ones, using regulation and skills rather than diagnosis and treatment. A coach doesn’t diagnose, prescribe, or treat mental illness — that’s a therapist’s work. A coach walks beside you in the doing.

What makes this different: I’m also a registered nurse (BSPH, BSN, RN) who trained in complex trauma and addiction — and then lived the far side of both. So I can speak the clinical language and tell you what the loop feels like from the inside. You are not broken. You are patterned. And patterns can change.

Who this is for

Is trauma recovery coaching right for me?

This work is for you if you understand your patterns and are done repeating them — the therapy veteran who is safe, functioning, and quietly exhausted by a loop she can name but can’t stop. If you are in acute crisis or need diagnosis and treatment, therapy or medical care comes first; coaching can walk beside it.

This is likely a fit if…

  • You’re high-functioning on the outside and still fighting the same loop inside.
  • You’ve done years of talk therapy and have the insight — but not the change.
  • You recognize the trauma–addiction overlap: to chaos, stress, work, a person, a substance.
  • Calm feels unfamiliar, even unsafe, and you want to learn to stay in it.
  • You’re ready to do practice between sessions, not just talk about it.

Something else may serve you better if…

  • You’re in acute crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself — please reach out to the crisis resources at the foot of this page.
  • You need a formal diagnosis, medication, or treatment for a mental-health condition.
  • You’re looking for someone to process the past in depth — that’s therapy’s lane.

Coaching is companionship alongside care — not a replacement for it.

The engine underneath

How does the Conscious Creation Method drive the work?

The loop runs faster than thought. So we don’t out-think it — we give it new evidence.

Every session is anchored in one named, teachable method that moves through three phases — Awareness, Regulation, Creation — in five steps. It’s what keeps the work from becoming one more open-ended conversation.

  1. Witness

    See the pattern without shame — name what the body is doing.

  2. Understand

    Trace the loop to its root: the wound beneath the behavior.

  3. Interrupt

    Meet the nervous system in the moment and offer it another option.

  4. Create

    Build new evidence — small, repeatable proof that safety is possible.

  5. Sustain

    Let the new pattern become the default. This is what lasting change feels like.

The question everyone asks

What’s the difference between a trauma coach and a therapist?

The honest version: a therapist can diagnose and treat mental-health conditions and works with the past clinically; a coach is a non-clinical guide who works present-and-forward on patterns, regulation, and action. Neither replaces the other — for many women, the two work best side by side. My RN background means I can hold the coaching lane while genuinely understanding the clinical one.

The investment

How much does trauma recovery coaching cost?

Honestly: it varies by container. Nationally, trauma recovery coaching commonly runs somewhere in the range of about $100 to $250 per session, with 1:1 packages priced as a whole arc and small groups costing meaningfully less per person. I’d rather tell you the truth than dangle a mystery — the exact figure for your path is shared plainly on your first call, before you commit to anything.

My philosophy on money is the same as my philosophy on recovery: no pressure, no scarcity theater, no urgency. Coaching is an investment in changing a pattern that has been quietly costing you far more — in energy, relationships, and time — than any package price. But it’s only worth it if it’s the right fit, and that’s exactly what the first call is for.

If the 1:1 investment isn’t right for you yet, the Small Group Healing Circle is the lower-cost door, and the free resources — the Trauma Tree Worksheet, the quiz, and the Sunday Letter — are a real place to begin at no cost at all.
