The short version

What is a trauma recovery coach?

A trauma recovery coach is a trained, non-clinical guide who helps you move present-and-forward: interrupting the patterns trauma left behind and building new ones, using regulation and skills rather than diagnosis and treatment. That’s a therapist’s work; a coach walks beside you in the doing.

What makes this different: I’m also a registered nurse (BSPH, BSN, RN) who trained in complex trauma and addiction, and then lived the far side of both. So I can speak the clinical language and tell you what the loop feels like from the inside. Your patterns are not a flaw in you, and patterns can change.

Who this is for

Is trauma recovery coaching right for me?

This work is for you if you understand your patterns and are done repeating them: the therapy veteran who is safe, functioning, and quietly exhausted by a loop she can name but can’t stop. If you are in acute crisis or need diagnosis and treatment, therapy or medical care comes first; coaching can walk beside it.

This is likely a fit if…

  • You’re high-functioning on the outside and still fighting the same loop inside.
  • You’ve done years of talk therapy and have the insight, but not the change.
  • You recognize the trauma–addiction overlap: to chaos, stress, work, a person, a substance.
  • Calm feels unfamiliar, even unsafe, and you want to learn to stay in it.
  • You’re ready to do practice between sessions, not just talk about it.

Something else may serve you better if…

  • You’re in acute crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself. Please reach out to the crisis resources at the foot of this page.
  • You need a formal diagnosis, medication, or treatment for a mental-health condition.
  • You’re looking for someone to process the past in depth, that’s therapy’s lane.

Coaching is companionship alongside care, not a replacement for it.

The work itself

What does working together actually look like?

Two ways in, one method underneath, and none of it is open-ended talk. Every path moves through the same five steps, each with a name, a purpose, and an endpoint, at the pace your nervous system can hold. Exact length, cadence, and investment are confirmed together on your first call, so you’re never guessing.

The ARC Program

1:1 trauma recovery coaching through the Conscious Creation Method™

The flagship 12-week container: private, weekly 1:1 sessions (about 60 minutes) across a focused arc from pattern to purpose. It moves in two phases, and includes between-session support and practices, with additional sessions available when you want to keep going.

  • Phase 1 · Breaking the Loop (weeks 1-6): Witness, Understand, Interrupt. See the pattern, name the story, and build the pause that interrupts the loop.
  • Phase 2 · Building Forward (weeks 7-12): Find Creative Flow, Build the Intentional Life. Create your way through it and author a life your nervous system can keep.

The engine underneath

How does the Conscious Creation Method drive the work?

The reaction arrives before the thinking mind does. So we don’t out-think it; we practice something new.

Every session is anchored in one named, teachable method that moves through the three ARC stages (A: Awareness, R: Regulation, C: Creation) in five steps, with creation as the central mechanism, not a side tool. Emerging research suggests that creative, arts-based practice can meaningfully reduce trauma symptoms. It’s what keeps the work from becoming one more open-ended conversation.

The question everyone asks

What’s the difference between a trauma coach and a therapist?

The honest version: a therapist can diagnose and treat mental-health conditions and works with the past clinically; a coach is a non-clinical guide who works present-and-forward on patterns, regulation, and action. Neither replaces the other; for many women, the two work best side by side.

Comparison of trauma recovery coaching and therapy across focus, scope, and approach
  Trauma recovery coaching (with an RN) Therapy
Primary focus Present and forward: interrupting patterns, building regulation and new responses. Can treat the past clinically: processing, symptom relief, healing wounds.
Diagnosis & treatment No. Coaches do not diagnose, prescribe, or treat mental illness. Yes. Licensed to diagnose and treat mental-health conditions.
The relationship A peer, companion, guide: walking beside you. A clinician–client relationship within a licensed scope.
How the work moves Action- and skills-oriented: practice between sessions, nervous-system tools, accountability. Varies by modality (CBT, EMDR, IFS, psychodynamic, and more).
Regulation & insurance Unregulated profession; not billable to medical insurance. Licensed and regulated; often insurance-eligible.
Best when… You have the insight and stability, and want to turn it into change. You need diagnosis, treatment, or to process trauma in depth.

The investment

How much does trauma recovery coaching cost?

Honestly: it varies by container, with 1:1 packages priced as a whole arc and small groups costing meaningfully less per person. The investment for your path is shared plainly before the fit call, with no pressure to continue. My philosophy on money matches my philosophy on recovery: no scarcity theater, no urgency.

Coaching is an investment in changing a pattern that has been quietly costing you far more (in energy, relationships, and time) than any package price. But it’s only worth it if it’s the right fit, and that’s exactly what the first call is for.

If the ARC Program investment isn’t right for you yet, The Healing Circle community is the free and low-cost place to gather, and the free resources (the Trauma Tree Worksheet, the quiz, and the Sunday Letter) are a real place to begin at no cost at all.
