A personalized path from pattern to purpose.

You have done the work. The loop is still running.

You have read the books. Done the therapy. Tried the apps, the workshops, the breathwork. And you are still reacting the same way to the same triggers. That is not failure. That is the loop.

I am a registered nurse, certified trauma recovery coach, and author of Addicted to Trauma. I help people who are insight-rich and still stuck interrupt the pattern at its source: through the body, not around it.

Insight alone does not always interrupt the pattern. You can create your way through it.

The plan

Three stages. One word to remember: ARC.

  • A · Awareness

    See the pattern. Name the story. You cannot change what you cannot see.

  • R · Regulation

    Build the pause. Interrupt the loop. The power lives in the twenty-second window between trigger and response.

  • C · Creation

    Find flow. Build the intentional life. The act of making can reach places insight alone has not.

How the twelve weeks move

Two phases. Weekly sessions. One arc.

  • Weeks 1 to 6 1

    Breaking the Loop

    Witness the pattern. Understand the story. Build the pause.

  • Weeks 7 to 12 2

    Building Forward

    Find creative flow. Build the intentional life.

Leave more connected to the truth of who you are before the patterns took over.

Questions, answered plainly

The honest fine print

How much does the ARC Program cost?

The ARC Program is $3,000, paid in full or as three monthly payments of $1,000. The total is the same either way. This is the launch price and it will rise after the book launches. Card payments carry a 3 percent processing fee, stated in the coaching agreement.

Is the ARC Program therapy?

No. The ARC Program is trauma recovery coaching and education. It is present-and-forward work on patterns, regulation, and creation. It is not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment, and it can walk beside care from a licensed professional without replacing it.

Who is the ARC Program not for?

This work asks for honesty and willingness. It is not the right season if you are in acute crisis, or if you need diagnosis or treatment first; licensed care comes before coaching, and I will say so plainly. And it is not a fit if you want the pattern named but not changed. Willingness to look at every version of yourself, with curiosity rather than judgment, is the only entry requirement.

What happens on the free discovery call?

The call is a conversation, not a pitch. You talk first. I listen, reflect back the pattern I hear, and explain how the program works. If it is a fit, I say so plainly. If it is not, I point you toward a better resource and leave the door open.
