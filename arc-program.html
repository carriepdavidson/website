How the twelve weeks move

Two phases, five steps, one method

The program runs on the Conscious Creation Method™: Awareness, Regulation, Creation. Not open-ended talk. Every session has a place in the arc, and the arc has an endpoint.

  • Phase 1 · Breaking the Loop (weeks 1 to 6)

    Witness, Understand, Interrupt. See the pattern clearly, name the story underneath it, and build the pause that interrupts the loop where it starts: in the nervous system, not the intellect.

  • Phase 2 · Building Forward (weeks 7 to 12)

    Find Creative Flow, Build the Intentional Life. Create your way through what opens up, and author a life your nervous system can actually keep, on purpose.

What’s included

What you actually get

  • Private 1:1 sessions with Carrie, 60 minutes, every week across the twelve weeks. Just the two of us, on the pattern you came to break.
  • Support between sessions. Voice-note and message access between calls, because the pattern does not wait for your next appointment.
  • A personalized workbook built around your pattern, your history, and your pace.
  • Creative healing assignments. Creation is the method’s engine, not a side activity: small, doable practices that let your hands teach your nervous system what safety feels like.
  • A clear agreement and a welcome packet before the first session: what to expect, how sessions work, and what materials to have on hand. No surprises.

Honest edges

Who this is for, and who it is not for

  • This is for you if

    You are insight-rich and transformation-poor: you understand your patterns and still can’t stop. You have likely done therapy and are grateful for it, and you want somewhere to take what you learned. You are willing to face every version of yourself with curiosity, including the ones you are not proud of. All of you is welcome here: every identity, every orientation, every version of you that shows up.

  • This is not for you if

    You want someone to agree that nothing can change. You are not ready to hear hard truths about yourself, even kindly delivered. You would rather judge than get curious. Or you are in acute crisis or need diagnosis and treatment: that is therapy’s work, and it comes first. Coaching can walk beside that care, and I will tell you honestly if I think it should.

The first conversation

What the discovery call actually is

A conversation, not a pitch. If a sales script would set your nervous system on edge, good news: there isn’t one. Here is exactly how the 45 to 60 minutes go, so there is nothing to brace for.

The first stretch is yours: what brought you, what you have tried, in your own words, uninterrupted. Then I reflect back the pattern I hear and ask if it lands. If it does, I explain how the ARC Program works, plainly. You ask anything, including the hard questions about what this work will ask of you, and I answer honestly.

“I don’t take everyone who reaches out. I want to make sure this is the right fit for both of us. If it is, I will say so plainly, show you what enrollment looks like, and give you room to decide.”

If it is not a fit, I will say that kindly and point you toward something better suited. Afterward you get one follow-up note summarizing what we talked about. No chase, no countdown, no pressure. Deciding slowly is allowed here.

Questions

The honest fine print

How much does the ARC Program cost?

$3,000 paid in full, or three monthly payments of $1,000. That is the whole price: nothing saved for the call, nothing to negotiate. Card payments carry a 3% processing fee, stated in the coaching agreement.

Is the ARC Program therapy?

No. This is trauma recovery coaching and education: non-clinical, present-and-forward work on patterns, regulation, and action. It is not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment. If you are working with a therapist or physician, this work can walk beside that care. It does not replace it.

What happens on the free discovery call?

You talk first, I listen. I reflect back the pattern I hear, explain how the program works, and answer every question honestly. If it is a fit I say so plainly; if not, I say that too and point you somewhere better. One gentle follow-up afterward, and that is all.

What if I am not ready yet?

Then start free, truly. The Sunday Letter, the 3-minute quiz, and the resources library are real places to begin, and the door to the program stays open.
