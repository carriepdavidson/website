Questions, answered plainly

About writing to me

How quickly will I hear back?

I read every message myself and answer most within two or three days. If your note is about working together, the discovery call page is the faster path, because it gathers what I need to answer you well the first time. Either way, a real reply comes from me.

Where do speaking and podcast invitations go?

Right here, or straight to coaching@carriepdavidson.com. Tell me the audience, the date, and what you want the room to walk away with. Keynotes, clinical training, and podcast interviews all start with the same short note, and I answer those personally too.

Is this the right place to ask about coaching?

You can ask anything here and I will answer. The faster door is the free discovery call, where you can choose a time or start with a written note built for that conversation. No pressure and no pitch either way; we simply find out whether it is a fit.

What if I am in crisis right now?

Email is the wrong tool for a crisis, because I cannot reach you fast enough. In the U.S., call or text 988, or text HOME to 741741. When things are steadier, my inbox will still be here, and I will still read every word you send.

If you are in crisis, reach out now

You deserve immediate support. In the U.S.: call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) · text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line) · SAMHSA National Helpline 1-800-662-4357. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

Coaching is companionship alongside care, not a replacement for it. This work is trauma recovery coaching and education. It is not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment, and it is not a substitute for care from a licensed mental-health or medical professional. If you are working with a therapist or physician, coaching can walk beside that care. It does not replace it.
