The Sunday Letter

A weekly letter for the work no one else sees.

Thoughts on trauma, creativity, healing, and becoming. In your inbox.

Every Sunday: neuroscience precision, a poetic reframe, and one small practice you can actually use. No hustle, no shame, no urgency. Just companionship on the way home to yourself.

Get the Sunday Letter, free

A confirmation email arrives first; click it and you are in. No spam. Unsubscribe anytime.

What to expect

What arrives on Sunday

  • One honest essay

    On trauma patterns, the nervous system, ADHD, and recovery. Written the way the coaching works: precise about the science, gentle about the person.

  • One small practice

    Something you can try the same day, in minutes. Not a program, not homework. A door left open.

  • First word on the memoir

    Subscribers hear it first when Addicted to Trauma has a date, an excerpt, or early copies. Joining the letter is joining the book waitlist.

  • Nothing else

    No spam, no funnels, no countdown timers. If a letter is not worth your Sunday, unsubscribe with one click and no hard feelings.

If you are in crisis, reach out now

You deserve immediate support. In the U.S.: call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) · text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line) · SAMHSA National Helpline 1-800-662-4357. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

Coaching is companionship alongside care, not a replacement for it. This work is trauma recovery coaching and education. It is not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment, and it is not a substitute for care from a licensed mental-health or medical professional. If you are working with a therapist or physician, coaching can walk beside that care. It does not replace it.
