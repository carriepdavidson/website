CPTSD & Trauma Patterns

The gold standard failed most of us. That's not the same as you failing.

You are not the failure. You are the two out of three.

By Carrie Davidson, BSPH, BSN, RN, Registered nurse and certified trauma recovery coach
Published July 11, 2026 · 5 min read

This article is trauma recovery education, not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment. It is not a substitute for care from a licensed mental-health or medical professional. If you are working with a therapist or physician, let this walk beside that care. It does not replace it.

No one tells you the number. They hand you the therapy like it's the cure and they let you believe that if it doesn't work, the broken thing is you.

So here's the number. The treatments everyone calls the gold standard for trauma. The ones with the acronyms. CPT, prolonged exposure, EMDR. The evidence-based, insurance-approved, this-is-what-the-research-says treatments. A 2026 review of veterans and military populations found they leave somewhere between 39 and 72 percent of people without meaningful relief. Read that again. Depending on the study, up to two out of three people do everything right, sit in every session, do the homework, relive the worst day of their life on purpose because a clinician told them to... and still don't get better.

And no one says that part out loud when they refer you.

I know that room. I have sat across from the well-meaning professional with the binder and the protocol and felt my whole body go quiet in the way that isn't calm, it's compliance. I did the thing. I white-knuckled through a modality that was supposed to touch it and it didn't touch it. And I walked out into the parking lot and thought, well. Even the thing that works doesn't work on me. There must be a special kind of broken that I am.

Here's what I know now that I'm a nurse and not just a patient. When they say a treatment is evidence-based, what that means is it worked for a statistically significant portion of a studied group. It does not mean it works for everyone. It does not even mean it works for most. Evidence-based is a floor, not a promise. And trauma, especially the complex kind, the kind that got wired in before you had words for it, the kind that came from the people who were supposed to keep you safe... that kind does not always respond to a protocol designed for a single terrible event. You cannot always exposure-therapy your way out of a nervous system that was built in a house where the danger never ended. There was no single incident to process. The incident was your childhood.

That's not a treatment failure. That's a map problem. The map was drawn for a different territory.

That's not a treatment failure. That's a map problem.

And this is the part I need you to actually hear, because I'm not saying it from the other side of it. I'm not healed. I don't know if I ever fully will be. I still have days the old wiring runs the whole show before I catch it. But I have stopped reading my own difficulty as my own defectiveness, and that one shift changed my life more than any single modality ever did. When the thing didn't work, I got taught to blame the only variable I couldn't switch out. Me. Nobody ever said maybe the protocol was too small for what happened to you. Maybe you needed more than eight sessions and a worksheet. Maybe the fact that it took you longer is information about the size of the wound, not the strength of your will.

Because here's the quiet violence in that number. Up to 72 percent of people don't get relief from the gold standard, and almost every one of them walks away thinking they're the exception, the hopeless one, the one even the good treatment gave up on. Millions of people carrying the same private verdict. I'm the one it doesn't work on. That's not millions of broken people. That's a gap in the science that they've made you swallow as a personal failing.

So if you have tried the recommended thing and the recommended thing didn't reach you. If you have done the therapy correctly and still can't breathe right in certain rooms. If you have started to believe you are the unfixable one because the fix everyone swears by slid right off you... I need you to know the data is on your side, not against you. You are not the failure. You are the two out of three. You are the majority. You are the enormous, uncounted majority they don't put on the pamphlet.

Keep looking. Try the next thing. The somatic thing, the parts thing, the body thing, the thing with no acronym yet. Not because you're broken and chasing a cure, but because the first map was too small and you deserve one drawn for your actual terrain.

This one's for everyone who did everything right and still didn't get better, and quietly decided that meant something about them. It didn't. It meant the wound was bigger than the worksheet. I'm still finding my own way through it. I would not lie to you about that. But I'm not asking you to hope for a cure. I'm asking you to stop firing yourself.

Carrie Davidson, BSPH, BSN, RN

Registered nurse and certified trauma recovery coach; the nurse who became her own patient. She writes about the overlap of complex trauma and addiction: the same wound, telling itself in two different languages. Author of the memoir Addicted to Trauma.
