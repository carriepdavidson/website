Nervous System Regulation

You Cannot Buy Your Way Back Into Your Body.

There is no reset. There is only relationship.

By Carrie Davidson, BSPH, BSN, RN, Registered nurse and certified trauma recovery coach
Published July 11, 2026 · 5 min read

This article is trauma recovery education, not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment. It is not a substitute for care from a licensed mental-health or medical professional. If you are working with a therapist or physician, let this walk beside that care. It does not replace it.

They're selling you a nervous system now.

Have you noticed? Scroll for thirty seconds and there it is. The little device that clips behind your ear and hums your vagus nerve into submission. The seven-day reset summit. The supplement stack. The breathwork subscription. 2026 has decided that the nervous system is the new gut, the new adrenals, the new thing that is broken in you and available for purchase. They're even calling it a category now. Neurowellness. The Great Nervous System Reset.

And I want to believe it. God, I want to believe it. Because I'm a woman who has spent actual money on the promise that safety comes in a box, and I'm a nurse who knows exactly why that promise doesn't hold.

Here's the thing nobody selling you the reset will say out loud. Your nervous system is not dysregulated because you haven't found the right gadget. It's dysregulated because at some point it learned that the world was not safe, and it was right. It adapted. It got very, very good at scanning a room, reading a face, catching the shift in someone's voice half a second before the yelling started. That's not a malfunction you reboot. That's a map that got written in childhood and has been running you ever since.

I know this in my body. Not as a concept. In my body.

I can sit in a quiet room, candle lit, everything technically fine, and my chest will not drop. My shoulders stay up around my ears like they're waiting for something. My eyes keep finding the exits. Nothing is wrong and my whole system is braced for the thing that isn't coming. That's hypervigilance, and it does not care that I paid for the app. The amygdala doesn't check my receipts. It learned danger before I had words, and it has been keeping me alive with that lesson for decades. You cannot Venmo your way out of that.

The amygdala doesn't check my receipts.

And there's a fight happening right now, in the actual research, about whether the polyvagal theory that half of this industry is built on even holds up the way people say it does. The scientists are arguing. Papers are getting picked apart. And meanwhile the marketing is selling certainty by the month. That gap should tell you something. When the people making money are more sure than the people doing the studies, the product is not the science. The product is your hope.

So let me say the true thing, as a nurse and as someone still climbing.

You cannot buy your way back into your body.

There is no reset. There is only relationship. Slow, unsexy, unmonetizable relationship. It looks like noticing you're braced and not hating yourself for it. It looks like putting a hand on your own chest and staying there when everything in you wants to bolt. It looks like letting your system be wrong about the danger a hundred times, gently, until one day it's wrong a little slower. That's not a seven-day summit. That's the work of a life. My life. I'm doing it right now, today, and I'm not on the other side of it.

I'll tell you where I actually am. I went from a version of me that was planning how to not exist to a version that looks forward to the morning. That's the data I trust. Not because a device measured my heart rate variability and gave me a green light. Because I lived it. My body is still learning that calm is not the quiet before something detonates. Some days it believes me. Some days it doesn't. I keep showing up to the conversation anyway.

And here's what I need you to hear, especially if you're the one with a cart full of regulation tools and a body that still won't settle. You are not behind. You didn't fail the reset. There was never a reset to pass. Your nervous system is not a problem to solve in a weekend. It's a survivor. It kept you here. The least we can do is stop trying to override it with a subscription and start learning to speak its language instead.

This one's for the women who bought the device and cried anyway. For the ones who did every technique and still couldn't breathe in their own kitchen. For the entrepreneurs building empires on a foundation their body never signed off on. You are not too broken for the fancy fix. You were never broken. You were adapted. And adaptation doesn't unwind with money. It unwinds with time, with truth, and with someone finally telling you the truth instead of selling you the shortcut.

I'm not healed. I don't know if I ever fully will be. But I have hope, and I have a body I'm learning to stop punishing.

That's not for sale. It was always yours.

Carrie Davidson, BSPH, BSN, RN

Registered nurse and certified trauma recovery coach; the nurse who became her own patient. She writes about the overlap of complex trauma and addiction: the same wound, telling itself in two different languages. Author of the memoir Addicted to Trauma.
