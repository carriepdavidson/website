Rooted & Rising · A 6-Week Conscious Creation Journey

Welcome back to yourself.

You cannot create a new life from a nervous system that is still bracing for the old one. So we begin with you.

You have arrived at the perfect moment. I'm Carrie, and I am so glad you're here.

Before anything else, here is the premise this whole course rests on: you cannot create a new life from a nervous system that is still bracing for the old one. Most manifestation teaching starts with the wishlist. Rooted & Rising starts with the woman doing the wishing. You don't attract the life you want. You grow the capacity to receive it, and then you act like the woman who already lives there.

Over the next six weeks we will move through six phases, in an order that matters: Ground, Clear, Vision, Embody, Act, Trust. We ground before we clear, because belief work done in survival mode just hurts. We clear before we vision, because you can't hear what you truly want over the noise of what you were told to want. We vision before we embody, we embody before we act, and we act before we trust, because surrender only means something after you've done your part.

HOW EACH WEEK WORKS

Every week has the same dependable shape, on purpose. Predictability is itself a kindness to a nervous system that grew up without much of it. You will find:

1. An opening letter from me that frames the week. 2. Three short teachings. 3. One signature practice, done once, deeply. 4. A daily anchor: a 10 to 15 minute ritual that evolves week to week. 5. An affirmation of the week, which you are encouraged to adapt until it lands as true in your body. 6. Journal prompts and a reflection page in your workbook.

A new week unlocks every seven days. That pacing is deliberate: this work compounds, and rushing it is the old way. Download your workbook below this lesson and keep it close; it is where the whole journey gets written down.

ONE HONEST NOTE BEFORE WE BEGIN

This course is education and practice, not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment, and it is not a substitute for care from a licensed mental-health or medical professional. If you are working with a therapist, this course can walk beside that care. It does not replace it. And if grounding or reflection ever stirs up more than feels safe to hold alone, that is not failure. It is information, and reaching for licensed support is a strong move, not a setback.

I'll see you in Week 1. We begin, of all places, in your body.
