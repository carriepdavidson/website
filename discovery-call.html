The first step

Request a free discovery call

A conversation, not a pitch. Choose a time that suits you, or start with a note. Both lead to the same unhurried call.

Choose a time

Pick whatever works. The call is free, 30 minutes, and there is nothing to bring except yourself.

Or start with a note

If choosing a time feels like too much today, tell me a little about where you are, in your own words. Nothing here is a test to pass, and nothing you write commits you to anything. It simply means our first conversation starts with me already knowing a piece of your story.

I reply here. Nothing else happens with it unless you choose to subscribe to the Sunday Letter separately.

No surprises

What happens after you press send

  • 1. I read it. Actually me.

    No assistant, no autoresponder sequence. If you wrote a note, I read your words and reply personally from coaching@carriepdavidson.com, usually within a day or two. If you booked a time directly, the call on your calendar is the reply.

  • 2. We talk, no script

    30 minutes, free. You talk first and I listen. I reflect back the pattern I hear, explain how the ARC Program works, and answer the hard questions honestly.

  • 3. A plain answer

    If it is a fit, I say so and show you what enrollment looks like. If it is not, I say that kindly and point you toward something better suited. I do not take everyone who reaches out.

  • 4. Room to decide

    One follow-up note summarizing what we talked about. No chase, no countdown, no pressure. Deciding slowly is allowed here.

If you are in crisis, reach out now

You deserve immediate support. In the U.S.: call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) · text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line) · SAMHSA National Helpline 1-800-662-4357. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

Coaching is companionship alongside care, not a replacement for it. This work is trauma recovery coaching and education. It is not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment, and it is not a substitute for care from a licensed mental-health or medical professional. If you are working with a therapist or physician, coaching can walk beside that care. It does not replace it.
