The Monthly Gathering · Free

You Don't Heal in Isolation.

A free monthly gathering for anyone in the work. Teaching, sharing, community.

Belonging. Connection. Creation. These are not destinations. They are how we build.

How it works

Once a month, we gather online. I teach from one piece of the work: the loop, the pause, the trauma tree, the method. Then the room opens. People who have done the work share. People who are new just listen. No purchase required. No one is called on.

The first gathering is in September, after the book launches.

Get the invitation

Leave your email and I will send you the invitation when the first gathering is scheduled. Nothing else arrives except the Sunday Letter, and you can leave anytime.

Questions, answered plainly

About the Gathering

How much does the Monthly Gathering cost?

Nothing. The Gathering is free and open to everyone, whether or not you have ever worked with me. No purchase is required, and no offer is hiding behind it. It exists because healing needs witnesses, and most people are healing alone.

Do I have to share?

No. People who have done the work share. People who are new just listen. No one is called on, no camera is required, and leaving early is allowed. Your nervous system sets the pace, and quiet attendance counts as attendance.

When is the first gathering?

September 2026, after the memoir launches on August 8. Leave your email above and I will send the invitation with the date, the time, and the link as soon as it is scheduled. Rooted & Rising students receive access automatically.

What happens at a gathering?

I teach for a short while from one piece of the work: the loop, the pause, the trauma tree. Then the room opens for sharing, the way a good meeting does. We end on something you can practice before we meet again.

If you are in crisis, reach out now

You deserve immediate support. In the U.S.: call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) · text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line) · SAMHSA National Helpline 1-800-662-4357. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

Coaching is companionship alongside care, not a replacement for it. This work is trauma recovery coaching and education. It is not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment, and it is not a substitute for care from a licensed mental-health or medical professional. If you are working with a therapist or physician, coaching can walk beside that care. It does not replace it.
