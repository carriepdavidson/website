Questions, answered plainly

Start with the questions everyone asks

What is the trauma loop?

The trauma loop is the cycle where an old wound drives a present reaction. A trigger fires, the nervous system responds the way it learned to long ago, and the same story repeats. You cannot think your way out, because the loop lives in the body, not the intellect.

How do you break a trauma pattern?

You break a pattern in three stages. Awareness: see the loop and name the story underneath it. Regulation: build the pause between trigger and response. Creation: make something new, because the act of making moves what talking cannot reach. That sequence is the Conscious Creation Method™.

What is the difference between trauma coaching and therapy?

Therapy often looks back to diagnose and treat. Coaching works present-and-forward on patterns, regulation, and building what comes next. I am a registered nurse and certified trauma recovery coach, not a therapist, and my coaching can walk beside therapy without replacing it.

Where do I start?

Start free. Take the three-minute pattern quiz, download the Trauma Tree Worksheet, or subscribe to the Sunday Letter. When you are ready for more, Rooted & Rising is the self-paced course and the ARC Program is private coaching.
