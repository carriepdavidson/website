You might recognize this

When the skills that keep you safe at work do not switch off

Not everyone in a caregiving role carries trauma, and this is not a diagnosis of you. But if some of this feels familiar, it might be worth your attention.

You might still be reading a room for danger hours after the shift ends. You might be the one everyone relies on, and quietly wonder who holds it for you.

You might come home with nothing left, and somewhere in there, the creative life you used to have has gone quiet. Useful started to feel like the same thing as worthy.

You are not doing it wrong. A nervous system that learned to stay ready does not stand down because the shift ended. It is doing what it was trained to do, until it is given new evidence.

A little language for it

There are names for some of what caregivers carry

  • Compassion fatigue

    The slow erosion of caring for people in pain, day after day.

  • Secondary traumatic stress

    Absorbing other people’s worst moments as part of the job.

  • Burnout

    Real, and not always the whole story.

Naming these is not a diagnosis, only language. And sometimes a quieter question sits underneath: was some of the vigilance already there, before the first shift? You do not have to know today. You only have to be willing to ask.

The way back

Creativity is how you find your way back to yourself

You cannot reason your way back from this. The return happens in the body, and for many of us it runs straight through the creative life that went quiet.

Creativity is the opposite of stuck. Making becomes the evidence that a different life is possible. It is not about becoming an artist; it is about coming back to the parts of you the job asked you to set down.

Where the edges are

What this is, and what it is not

I want the boundary to be clear, because you of all people know why it matters.

This is coaching and education, present-and-forward companionship as you build a new pattern at the pace your nervous system can hold. My RN background informs how I understand trauma and the body, but coaching is not nursing, medical treatment, or clinical care, and it does not diagnose or treat anything. If you are in acute crisis, or you need diagnosis, medication, or treatment, licensed care comes first, and coaching can walk beside it.

This work is trauma recovery coaching and education. It is not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment, and it is not a substitute for care from a licensed mental-health or medical professional. It can walk beside that care. It does not replace it. If you are in crisis, the resources at the bottom of this page can help right now.

If any of this sounds like you

Start gently, wherever you are

You do not have to be ready. You have to be willing. The Sunday Letter is the easiest place to begin: a weekly note that pairs neuroscience with a gentler way home, written in the same voice as this page. When you want more, The ARC Program is the 1:1 coaching container, and Befriending Your Nervous System is a free workbook you can start today.
