# SEO & Search-Language Research — Carrie Davidson

*Compiled 2026-07-12. Purpose: map how Carrie's target customers actually type their pain and intent into search, so the rebuild's page copy, headlines, blog, and FAQ can meet them in their own words without losing her voice. Based on 15+ live web searches (July 2026).*

**Target recap:** high-achieving women, therapy veterans who are "insight-rich and transformation-poor," plus adjacent CPTSD / trauma-addiction overlap / ADHD-nervous-system audiences. Thesis: trauma and addiction are the same wound in two languages. Method: Witness → Understand → Interrupt → Create → Sustain.

**Headline signal:** "nervous system regulation" search interest is up ~1,000% year-over-year across Google/TikTok/Instagram (Spate data via NewBeauty/Forbes), named a top-2026 wellness trend, with "cortisol" searches at an all-time high. Carrie is positioned in a category that is exploding right now — the strategic play is to capture that surging generic demand and route it to her specific trauma-addiction frame. Sources: https://www.newbeauty.com/view/nervous-system-regulation , https://www.forbes.com/sites/meggenharris/2026/06/21/why-nervous-system-regulation-has-become-wellnesss-latest-obsession/

## (a) Primary Keyword Clusters with Intent Notes

### Cluster 1 — Coach vs. therapist / decision-stage (HIGH commercial intent)
The single most valuable cluster: someone actively deciding whether to hire a coach. They are past awareness and shopping.
- `trauma recovery coach vs therapist` / `trauma coaching vs therapy` / `difference between trauma coach and therapist`
- `what is a trauma recovery coach`
- `how much does a trauma recovery coach cost` / `is a trauma recovery coach worth it`
- `when to choose coaching for trauma instead of therapy`
- **Intent:** comparison + qualification. They want permission that coaching is "real" and to understand the boundary (coaches don't diagnose/treat; work present-and-forward; peer power dynamic; action/skills-oriented). This is where Carrie's RN credential is a massive trust differentiator — she can speak the clinical language while occupying the coaching lane.
- Sources: https://www.thetraumarecoverycoach.com/therapy-vs-coaching , https://certifiedtraumarecoverycoaching.com/what-is-a-trauma-recovery-coach , https://www.ziprecruiter.com/Salaries/Trauma-Recovery-Coach-Salary

### Cluster 2 — Nervous system regulation / somatic (HIGH volume, surging, top-of-funnel)
- `nervous system regulation` / `how to regulate your nervous system`
- `nervous system regulation coach` / `nervous system coaching for women`
- `somatic coaching` / `somatic coaching for women` / `what is somatic coaching`
- `stuck in fight or flight` / `how to get out of survival mode` / `nervous system stuck on high alert`
- `how to feel safe in your body`
- **Intent:** solution-seeking, body-based. Language repeatedly frames the problem as "mindset reframes don't work" and "your body holds what thought can't shift" — this dovetails exactly with Carrie's "You do not have to think your way out. You have to feel your way home."
- Sources: https://www.jennischanschieff.com/blog/what-is-somatic-coaching , https://sereniumwellness.com/stuck-in-survival-mode/ , https://blog.inthecortex.com/stuck-fight-or-flight/

### Cluster 3 — Trauma-addiction overlap / "addicted to chaos" (Carrie's DIFFERENTIATOR)
This is the cluster where she can own territory competitors don't touch. Real, high-emotion phrasing already exists in market:
- `addicted to chaos` / `addicted to drama` / `addicted to stress`

- `why does calm feel uncomfortable` / `why does peace feel boring`
- `trauma and addiction connection` / `addiction as a symptom of trauma` / `self-medicating trauma`
- **Intent:** self-diagnosis of a pattern they can't name. The market already describes it as "your nervous system mistakes drama for home" and "chaos addiction is a trauma response, not a personality flaw" — near-verbatim to Carrie's "the loop is a nervous system doing what it was trained to do." Her book title *Addicted to Trauma* IS this search cluster.

## (c) The Vocabulary Gap — Poetic Brand Language vs. Search Language (and how to bridge)

Carrie's voice is her moat, but **search engines and anxious searchers type plain, literal, symptom-first language.** Her signature lines are metaphor-forward; nobody searches "walking home" or "feel your way home." The bridge is a **two-layer copy pattern**: literal search phrase as the visible anchor (H1/H2, title tag, meta), poetic line immediately beneath as the emotional payoff. Never sacrifice the poetry — subordinate it, don't delete it.

| Brand / poetic language (KEEP as payoff) | How customers actually search (LEAD with) | Bridge move |
|---|---|---|
| "The loop runs faster than thought" | "why do I keep repeating the same patterns" | H2 = the search phrase; the poetic line becomes the subhead/opening sentence |
| "Insight-rich and transformation-poor" | "I understand my trauma but can't change" / "why isn't therapy working" | Use searcher phrase as the page title; her phrase as the memorable reframe in body + as pull-quote |
| "You have to feel your way home" | "how to regulate your nervous system" / "somatic healing" | Name the mechanism (nervous system/somatic) in the H1, land the poetry as the close |
| "You are not broken. You are patterned." | "can you have trauma and still be high functioning" / "why do I feel broken" | Mirror the literal fear ("broken") then deliver the reframe |
| "Addicted to Trauma" (book/thesis) | "addicted to chaos / stress / drama," "trauma and addiction connection" | Her title already IS a search term — lean in; build a pillar page around "addicted to chaos" that resolves into the book |
| "The nurse who became her own patient" | "trauma recovery coach," "trauma-informed coach RN" | Keep as brand story; add plain credential keywords (registered nurse, trauma recovery coach) in metadata/bio for E-E-A-T |
| "Finally feel at home in your body" | "how to feel safe in your body," "stuck in survival mode" | Literal H2, poetic H1 or vice-versa — test |

**Rule of thumb:** every core page should be findable by a phrase a scared, tired woman would actually type at 11pm, and memorable by a line only Carrie would write. Title tags and first H1 = search language. Everything else = full voice.

**Bonus:** Carrie's clinical precision (nervous system, HPA axis, cortisol, implicit memory, bottom-up processing) is itself search-friendly AND trust-building — the market rewards the exact neuro-vocabulary she already uses. She does not have to choose between poetic and precise; she should drop the *generic* wellness fluff, not the science.

## (d) Competitor Positioning Notes

**Annie Wright, LMFT (anniewright.com) — the closest and most dangerous competitor.** Occupies nearly identical ground: "driven / high-achieving women," "trauma-informed executive coaching," nervous-system framing, and *the same signature moves* — "insight is not enough," "you are not broken," "finally feel as good as your résumé looks," "over-functioning is a trauma response." She ranks deep across Clusters 4 and 5 (multiple indexed articles on CPTSD in driven women, intellectualization, high-achieving women + trauma).
- **Her edge:** 15,000+ clinical hours, LMFT license, prolific SEO content library, established domain authority.
- **Carrie's counter-position (differentiators to press):** (1) **the addiction axis** — Annie does "chaos" but does NOT own the trauma=addiction-are-one-wound thesis; Carrie's memoir + "Addicted to Trauma" title owns Cluster 3 outright. (2) **Lived experience + RN** — "the nurse who became her own patient" is a memoir-backed, first-person story Annie (clinician-observer) can't claim. (3) **A named, teachable Method** (Witness→Understand→Interrupt→Create→Sustain) vs. Annie's more open-ended therapy/coaching. (4) **The creative/"Conscious Creation" layer** — nobody else in this set pairs regulation with creation. Do not try to out-rank Annie on generic "high-achieving women trauma"; win the *addiction-overlap* and *book* long-tails where she's absent.

**Other players & how they position (brief):**
- **International Assoc. of Trauma Recovery Coaching / thetraumarecoverycoach.com / certifiedtraumarecoverycoaching.com** — own "what is a trauma recovery coach" and "coach vs therapist" (Cluster 1) definitionally; they're certification bodies, not personal brands. Carrie should produce her own coach-vs-therapist page with her RN lens to compete on trust, not just definition.
- **Nervous-system/somatic coaches (Sheridan Ruth, Jessica Maguire, Releaseology, Jenni Schanshieff)** — own "somatic coaching / nervous system regulation for women / high-functioning burnout." High-volume, crowded, generic. Enter via the trauma-addiction and book angle, not head-on.
- **Michelle Grosser, AK Psychotherapy, LifeCare** — rank for "cortisol addiction / addicted to stress." Cluster 3 is winnable but has emerging competition — move fast with a pillar page.
- **Memoir comps (not competitors, positioning anchors):** Stephanie Foo *What My Bones Know*, Gabor Maté *In the Realm of Hungry Ghosts*, Sarah Hepola *Blackout*, Pete Walker *Complex PTSD*. Position *Addicted to Trauma* explicitly against these on Amazon/Goodreads/book pages.
