Long bio

Carrie Davidson is the nurse who became her own patient. A registered nurse (BSPH, BSN, RN) and certified trauma recovery coach, she was trained in the science of both trauma and addiction, then lived the far side of both. That double vantage point, clinical training and lived-experience recovery, is the foundation of her work.

Her central thesis is that trauma and addiction are not separate problems. They are the same wound, telling itself in two different languages: the wound comes first, and the addiction is its result. She teaches this through the Conscious Creation Method, a five-step approach (Witness, Understand, Interrupt, Find Creative Flow, Build the Intentional Life) that helps high-achieving women interrupt the patterns trauma left behind and give the nervous system new evidence rather than one more retelling.

Carrie founded Conscious Coaching Collective, PLLC, where her coaching, her writing, and her free resources share one thesis. Her memoir, Addicted to Trauma, releases in Summer 2026. Her work is trauma recovery coaching and education; it is not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment.

The book

Addicted to Trauma: summary

Addicted to Trauma is a memoir built on a single, unifying idea: trauma and addiction are not two separate problems. They are the same wound, telling itself in two different languages, and the wound comes first. Carrie writes from both sides of that sentence: as a registered nurse trained to recognize the clinical machinery, and as a woman who lived the loop from the inside.

It is a redemption story, but not a tidy one: no single rock bottom, no lightning-bolt recovery, but the slower, truer work of coming home to yourself. Hardcover memoir, releasing Summer 2026. For readers of What My Bones Know and In the Realm of Hungry Ghosts.
