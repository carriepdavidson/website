Speaking

Topics Carrie speaks and writes about

Every topic comes from the work itself: the memoir, the method, and both sides of the chart. For stages, podcasts, panels, and trainings.

Trauma and addiction as one wound

The unified thesis: addiction understood not as a moral failure but as a symptom of an unfelt wound, the same wound telling itself in two languages.

Staying human in an increasingly automated world

The soul is the point, and some things were never meant to be automated: why creativity, regulation, and human presence matter more, not less, as the world speeds up.

High-functioning trauma

Why the more capable you look, the more invisible the suffering becomes, even to you, and what it takes to finally become your own patient.

The nurse who became her own patient

A lived-experience story told with clinical fluency: understanding your patterns perfectly and still being unable to stop.

Nervous-system regulation: why insight isn’t enough

Self-aware and still stuck: how bottom-up, body-based work gives a dysregulated nervous system new evidence that safety is possible.

ADHD or trauma?

How nervous-system dysregulation can look like ADHD, and why the distinction matters for how people understand themselves.

Common questions

What hosts and organizers ask

What topics does Carrie Davidson speak on?

Breaking the trauma loop, creative healing and nervous system regulation, trauma and addiction as one wound telling itself in two languages, and the real cost of caregiving for nurses and healthcare workers. She delivers science-backed insight with a story audiences do not forget.

What formats does she offer?

Keynotes, workshops, podcast interviews, panels, and clinical training, in person or virtual. Every talk is tailored to the room. Audiences leave with a fresh lens on trauma and patterns, and one practice they can use the same day.

How do you book Carrie Davidson for an event or podcast?

Write to coaching@carriepdavidson.com with the event or show, the audience, the date, and the format. The press kit on this page holds the short bio, boilerplate, and headshot notes, ready to send to a producer or program chair.

What makes her perspective different?

She has been on both sides of the chart. Carrie Davidson spent two years as a cardiac nurse reading vital signs, then became the patient who could not remember her own name. She speaks from lived recovery, grounded in clinical training and a method built from it.

If you are in crisis, reach out now

You deserve immediate support. In the U.S.: call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) · text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line) · SAMHSA National Helpline 1-800-662-4357. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.

Coaching is companionship alongside care, not a replacement for it. This work is trauma recovery coaching and education. It is not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment, and it is not a substitute for care from a licensed mental-health or medical professional. If you are working with a therapist or physician, coaching can walk beside that care. It does not replace it.
