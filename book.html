About the book

A story about the loop, and the way out.

The woman who signed the admission paperwork called herself Grace. She was not pretending.

Addicted to Trauma is the story of how a woman loses herself one small leaving at a time, and what it takes to find her way back.

It is not a memoir about rock bottom. It is about the floors beneath the floor you thought was the bottom, and the work of building something that holds.

For anyone who has ever mistaken chaos for love, busyness for healing, or someone else’s needs for their own. This book was written for you.

A note before you read on: this page holds the heaviest part of my story. Take it at your own pace. If it stirs more than it settles, 988 is there any hour, and the full list of supports is at the bottom of this page.

An excerpt

I had been not-quite-Carrie for a very long time before I became Grace. This is the story of how that happened.

If you are in crisis, reach out now

You deserve immediate support. In the U.S.: call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) · text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line) · SAMHSA National Helpline 1-800-662-4357. If you are in immediate danger, call 911.
