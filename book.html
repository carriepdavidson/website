From the prologue

Read the opening pages

The hallways were so white.

That is what I remember most. The walls, the ceiling, the floor, the doors. White in a way that meant I was not in the world I came from anymore.

I was wearing paper scrubs and a sheet wrapped around my shoulders. The phone was hung on the wall in the hallway. The communal kind, because the patients were not allowed to have their own. I do not remember who told me I could use it. I do not remember walking to it. I just remember standing there with the receiver in my hand and dialing my mother.

She did not answer.

I left a voicemail. I said the words I had been arranging in my head all morning.

Hey mom, it’s me. I just want you to know I’m okay.

And then, because she needed to know who was calling, I said the name I believed was mine.

I said Grace.

·  ·  ·

This is the story of how that happened. How I lost myself one small leaving at a time until I lost the part of me that knew my own name.

And how I found my way back.

You do not have to think your way out. You have to feel your way home.

Who this book is for

You have read the memoirs. You know the science.

You have the vocabulary, the ACE score, the attachment theory. And you are still waiting for something to change at the level where it actually lives. In your body. In your choices. In the quiet moments when the old pattern shows up again.

Addicted to Trauma is the story of what it took to stop running the patterns that almost killed me. I write from the floor I once found myself on, now standing. This is not a before-and-after. It is proof that the loop can be interrupted, and a map of what that looks like from the inside.

If you have been waiting for someone who has actually been there to show you the way out, this is the book.

About the book

What is Addicted to Trauma about?

Addicted to Trauma is my memoir about trauma and addiction as one wound telling itself in two languages. It is the story of how I lost myself one small leaving at a time, became the patient in the white hallway, and found my way back. It releases August 8, 2026.

When does the book release?

Addicted to Trauma releases August 8, 2026. Pre-ordering is the single most helpful thing a reader can do for a first book. Joining the waitlist gets you the release news, the pre-order link the day it exists, and the full first chapter.

Is it about addiction or trauma?

Both, because they are not separate problems. Trauma comes first and addiction is its result: the same wound telling itself in two different languages. The book traces that loop from the inside, without asking you to choose which story counts.

Where can I buy Addicted to Trauma?

Pre-order links go live on this page the moment they arrive from my publisher. Until then, join the waitlist and I will send you the link the day it exists. The memoir will be available in print and ebook.
