The Trauma Tree Worksheet

See the loop as one whole thing, not a hundred separate failures.

A free download for the Awareness stage of the Method. It gives you one place to map the roots (what happened), the trunk (the beliefs that grew from it), and the branches (the patterns you live out today), so a lifetime of separate failures can start to look like a single, understandable system.

Download the worksheet (free) →

The download is open, no email required. If you would like the Sunday Letter alongside it, a weekly note with one small practice, you can leave your email here. Only if and when you want it.

What it is

A guided worksheet for tracing one recurring pattern back to the wound beneath it. You work down the tree from the branch you can see (the behavior) to the root you usually cannot (what happened, and what your nervous system learned from it). Naming the whole tree is the first step of the Method: you cannot interrupt a loop you have never seen clearly.

Who it may help

It may help you if you understand your patterns but still cannot seem to break them: the insight-rich and still stuck. It is written for anyone tired of the loop, whether or not you would call what happened to you trauma. There are no wrong answers, and nothing here is a diagnosis.

What is included

A printable PDF you can also fill in on screen, with prompts for the roots, the trunk, and the branches, and space to trace one pattern from the belief underneath it to the behavior on top. Work through it in one sitting or return to it slowly, at your own pace.
