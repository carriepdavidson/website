About Carrie

A nurse who has been the patient.

I’m Carrie Davidson. For years I was the nurse reading the chart and, quietly, the patient inside the chart. I coach from that place: trauma experienced, not just trauma‑informed.

I don’t teach this work from the outside. I lived the loop, and I found my way out of it. That’s what I bring to the room.

My story

I knew the science. I was still running the pattern.

For years I could explain exactly what was happening to me. I had the language, the nursing training, the insight. And I was still living the same story on repeat, watching myself react in ways I swore I’d changed.

That’s the cruel part of a trauma pattern: understanding it isn’t the same as being free of it. The loop doesn’t live in the intellect. It lives in the body, in a nervous system that learned, long ago, how to keep me safe.

What finally moved things wasn’t more insight. It was learning to work through the body, to build a real pause between the trigger and the old response, and to make something new inside that pause.

I wrote Addicted to Trauma about what it took. Now I do this work alongside people who are done surviving the same story and ready to build a different one.

You are not broken. You are patterned. And patterns can change.

What I believe

The convictions this work is built on.

You are not broken.

Everything you survived taught your nervous system how to protect you. That isn’t damage. It’s a pattern, and patterns can change.

Through the body, not around it.

You can’t think your way out of a loop that lives below thought. The work happens where the pattern actually lives.
