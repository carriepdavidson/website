Sound familiar?

You’ve sat across from good therapists. You’ve journaled, read, named your patterns in language that would impress a clinician. You are not new to this work. You’re exhausted by it.

The problem was never your effort. Insight lives in the thinking brain, and the pattern lives in the body. The body moves first.

You are not broken. You are patterned. What looks like a character flaw is a nervous system doing exactly what it was trained to do, until you give it new evidence.

The nurse who became her own patient

I learned to read a room for danger before I learned to read a book. That vigilance made me an exceptional nurse. It also nearly cost me my life. Then came the day I became the patient in the white hallway who could not remember her own name.

I have been the nurse reading the chart and the patient inside the chart. Both sides of it are what I bring to this work.

What people ask me

Are you a therapist?

No. I am a registered nurse and certified trauma recovery coach. Coaching is present-and-forward work on patterns, regulation, and creation. It is not therapy, diagnosis, or medical treatment, and it can walk beside care from a licensed professional without replacing it.

What does trauma experienced mean?

Every practitioner in this space claims to be trauma-informed. I am trauma experienced. I did not study trauma and apply frameworks to other people. I ran the patterns through my own body, came out the other side, and built a method from what actually worked.

What is the Conscious Creation Method?

The Conscious Creation Method™ is my framework for breaking the trauma loop through nervous system regulation and creative healing. Three stages: Awareness, Regulation, Creation. Five steps underneath: witness, understand, interrupt, find creative flow, and build the intentional life.

How do we start working together?

Start with a free discovery call. It is a conversation, not a pitch. You talk first, I listen, and I reflect back the pattern I hear. If the ARC Program is a fit, I say so plainly. If you want to begin on your own, Rooted & Rising is self-paced.
